\begin{block}{Motivation \& Research Question}
  Large Language Models (LLMs) deliver strong reasoning, but at high \textbf{cost},
  \textbf{latency}, \textbf{energy}, and hardware demand. Small Language Models (SLMs)
  are cheap and edge-deployable, yet weaker on hard tasks. This project asks:

  \vspace{6pt}
  \begin{center}
  \begin{tikzpicture}
    \node[noveltybox, text width=0.9\linewidth] {
      \textit{When can collaborative SLM--LLM architectures preserve LLM-grade
      answer quality while substantially cutting cost, latency, energy, and CO\textsubscript{2}
      compared with a monolithic LLM?}
    };
  \end{tikzpicture}
  \end{center}

  \vspace{6pt}
  \textbf{Contributions.}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item A reproducible \textbf{experiment platform} that serves models across three GPU
          tiers and measures latency, tokens, cost, energy, and emissions per request.
    \item A fixed \textbf{35-run monolithic sweep} used to populate reusable benchmark
          baselines and to compare dense LLMs against MoE variants under identical defaults.
    \item A family of orchestration \textbf{architectures}, including \textbf{novel decentralized
          ``swarm'' designs} not present in the literature.
    \item \textbf{EATS}, a single efficiency-aware quality score, plus a planned
          coding-focused benchmark for architecture-level comparison.
  \end{itemize}
\end{block}
