\begin{block}{EATS: Efficiency-Aware Task Score}
  A single score that rewards architectures preserving answer quality while
  avoiding unnecessary cost, algorithmic latency, and energy.

  \vspace{4pt}
  \begin{center}
  \begin{tikzpicture}
    \node[draw=posterTeal, fill=posterTeal!12, rounded corners=6pt, inner sep=12pt,
          text width=0.88\linewidth, align=center] {
      \[
        \mathrm{EATS} \;=\;
        \frac{\text{accuracy}}
             {\text{accuracy} +
              0.5\,\text{normalized\_cost} +
              0.3\,\text{normalized\_algorithmic\_latency} +
              0.2\,\text{normalized\_energy}}
      \]
    };
  \end{tikzpicture}
  \end{center}

  \vspace{2pt}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{accuracy} --- fraction of correct answers.
    \item \textbf{normalized\_cost} --- total cost $\div$ full-LLM-baseline cost.
    \item \textbf{normalized\_algorithmic\_latency} --- average intrinsic inference-and-orchestration latency $\div$ full-LLM-baseline value.
    \item \textbf{normalized\_energy} --- total energy $\div$ full-LLM-baseline energy.
  \end{itemize}
  Higher is better: the additive weighted penalty suppresses systems that are costly, slow, or
  energy-hungry without collapsing when any single dimension is unmeasured.
  Weights sum to 1 (cost 50\%, latency 30\%, energy 20\%). The score is bounded to $[0,1]$.
  Structurally analogous to the F-score: accuracy / (accuracy + penalty).

  \vspace{4pt}
  \textbf{Measured per request} via a vLLM metrics proxy (\texttt{\_thesis} block):
  latency, input/output tokens, infra cost, \texttt{energy\_kwh}, and
  \texttt{co2\_g} (grid factor $380$\,g\,CO\textsubscript{2}/kWh by default).
\end{block}
