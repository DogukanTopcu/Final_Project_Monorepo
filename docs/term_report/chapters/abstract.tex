\chapter*{Abstract}

Large language models (LLMs) achieve state-of-the-art performance on reasoning and language understanding tasks but impose substantial inference costs in latency, energy, and monetary expenditure. Recent research explores collaborative architectures that combine small language models (SLMs) with LLMs through routing, speculative decoding, ensemble coordination, and decentralized swarm strategies to reduce these costs. However, the field still lacks a systematic evaluation framework that treats cost and energy as first-class outcomes alongside accuracy. This gap was identified through a companion systematic literature review of 54 collaborative SLM--LLM studies conducted under a PRISMA 2020-guided protocol, and it directly motivated the measurement-first design of the platform presented in this report.

The platform executes every architecture under a shared contract that records accuracy, latency, cost, energy, and carbon for every query. A composite metric, EATS (Efficiency-Accuracy Trade-off Score), is introduced to rank architectures jointly on quality and efficiency. The experimental study evaluates the complete architecture taxonomy---standalone, routing, speculative decoding, debate, active oracle, blackboard, entropy blackboard, ensemble, and pure swarm---across five public benchmarks. On MMLU, routing reaches 76.7\% accuracy while limiting heavy-model invocations to 48.7\% of queries, and speculative decoding reaches 86.0\%. On ARC, HellaSwag, and TruthfulQA, routing and speculative decoding remain near the top of the efficiency--quality frontier, achieving accuracy within a few percentage points of the best standalone models while reducing per-query cost and energy by substantial margins. Pure Swarm reaches the highest accuracy on one benchmark using no heavy-model calls, while multi-SLM ensembles come within one point of the best coordination-based accuracy on another, at the cost of elevated latency. The two novel decentralised architectures, Blackboard and Entropy Blackboard, resolve 72--93\% of ARC and GSM8K queries on SLM workers alone at up to 97\% accuracy---the highest raw accuracy in the study---and the uncertainty-penalised bidding rule of Entropy Blackboard recovers three to seven accuracy points on benchmarks where SLM confidence is poorly calibrated while making the architecture largely insensitive to the choice of task-claiming policy. EATS yields a consistent ranking signal across the evaluated benchmarks, capturing these trade-offs in a single composite score.

The evaluation covers five general-purpose language benchmarks: MMLU, ARC-Challenge, GSM8K, HellaSwag, and TruthfulQA, and now spans every architecture defined in the taxonomy. The principal planned extensions are deeper sample counts for the most recently benchmarked architectures and sensitivity analyses of their orchestration parameters.
