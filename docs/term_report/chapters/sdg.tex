\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{ALIGNMENT WITH UN SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT GOALS}

This project does not target a single social sector such as healthcare or education. Its contribution is infrastructural: it improves how language-model systems are evaluated, compared, and governed. That still creates meaningful alignment with sustainable development goals because the platform makes hidden resource trade-offs visible and helps avoid wasteful deployment choices.

\begin{table}[htbp]
    \centering
    \caption{Alignment of the thesis project with selected UN SDGs.}

    \begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{} l l X @{}}

        \midrule
        Social (SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities) & Indirect & By comparing smaller and larger model architectures under a common framework, the project supports more accessible AI deployment paths for teams that cannot afford frontier-scale hardware or API usage. \\
        Health (SDG 3: Good Health and Well-being) & Indirect & The project reduces engineering uncertainty and manual experimentation overhead by providing a structured platform for comparison, reporting, and reproducibility. \\
        Security (SDG 16: Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions) & Indirect & The platform encourages transparent reporting of model behavior, host usage, and benchmark assumptions, which supports more accountable evaluation practices. \\
        Economic (SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth) & Direct & Cost-aware orchestration and shared-host visibility help teams reason about whether a workload really requires an expensive LLM, which can improve engineering productivity and reduce unnecessary spending. \\
        Sustainability and environment (SDGs 12 and 13) & Direct & Energy, carbon, and EATS reporting are central to the platform. The project explicitly tracks \texttt{energy\_kwh} and \texttt{co2\_g} so that model quality can be discussed alongside environmental cost. \\
